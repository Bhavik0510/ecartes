Why E-invoice

In India, e-invoicing is mandatory for businesses with a turnover exceeding Rs. 5 crores. The e-invoice must follow the requirements as per the Goods and Services Tax (GST) Act, and must be generated and transmitted in a structured electronic format. Here are some key requirements for e-invoicing in India:
E-Invoice Format: The e-invoice must be generated in the JSON (JavaScript Object Notation) format, which is a structured electronic format that follows the GST rules.
Invoice Reference Number (IRN): Each e-invoice must have a unique Invoice Reference Number (IRN) assigned by the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP).
Quick Response (QR) Code: The e-invoice must have a QR code containing key details of the invoice, such as the GSTIN of the supplier and the buyer, the invoice number and date, and the invoice amount.
Supplier Details: The supplier's name, address, GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number), and contact information must be mentioned.
Customer Details: The customer's name, address, GSTIN (if registered), and contact information must be mentioned.
Item Details: A description of the goods or services provided, the quantity, and the price per unit must be mentioned.
Tax Details: The GST rate and the amount of GST charged for each item must be mentioned.
Total Invoice Amount: The total amount payable must be mentioned.
Payment Terms: The payment terms and due date must be mentioned.
Digital Signature: The e-invoice must be digitally signed by the supplier or an authorized representative.
It is important to note that e-invoicing in India requires integration with the GST portal and the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP). Businesses must also maintain proper records and ensure compliance with GST regulations.
In summary, e-invoicing in India is mandatory for businesses with a turnover exceeding Rs. 5 crores, and must follow the requirements as per the GST Act. The e-invoice must be generated and transmitted in a structured electronic format, and must include key details such as the IRN, QR code, supplier and customer details, item and tax details, and digital signature.

Why E-WayBill

An E-Way bill is an electronic document that is required for the movement of goods worth over Rs. 50,000 in India. It is a unique document that contains details such as the goods being transported, the value of the goods, the consignor and consignee details, and the transporter details.
The E-Way bill was introduced by the Government of India to simplify the process of transporting goods across different states in the country. The bill is generated online through the GST portal or other approved platforms, and is used by authorities to verify the movement of goods and ensure compliance with tax regulations.
Once generated, the E-Way bill must be carried by the transporter or the person in charge of the conveyance. It is valid for a certain period of time depending on the distance of transportation, and must be updated with any changes or additional information as required.
In summary, the E-Way bill is an electronic document that is required for the movement of goods worth over Rs. 50,000 in India. It contains details such as the goods being transported, the value of the goods, the consignor and consignee details, and the transporter details. The bill is generated online and is used to ensure compliance with tax regulations and verify the movement of goods.
